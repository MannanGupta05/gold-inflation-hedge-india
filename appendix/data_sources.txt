DATA SOURCES FOR "IS GOLD A RELIABLE INFLATION HEDGE IN INDIA? (2015–2025)"

1. CONSUMER PRICE INDEX (CPI) – INDIA

   • Variable: All-India CPI (Combined), Base 2012 = 100
   • Frequency: Monthly
   • Coverage: January 2015 – January 2025

   Primary Source:
   • Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MOSPI)
     - Portal: CPI Data Portal
     - URL (entry point): https://cpi.mospi.gov.in

   Procedure:
   • Select All-India CPI (Combined) series
   • Set date range: Jan 2015 – Jan 2025
   • Export to CSV, with columns:
     - Date (YYYY-MM-DD, first day of month)
     - CPI_Combined

   Notes:
   • This is the headline CPI series used by the Reserve Bank of India for inflation targeting.[web:24][web:75]


2. GOLD PRICE DATA – INDIA (MCX)

   • Variable: MCX Gold Price (INR per 10 grams), near‑month futures
   • Frequency: Daily (aggregated to monthly)
   • Coverage: January 2015 – January 2025

   Primary Source:
   • Investing.com – Gold Historical Data (India / MCX)
     - URL (entry point): https://in.investing.com/commodities/gold-historical-data

   Procedure:
   • On the page, select:
     - Instrument: Gold (MCX)
     - Date range: 01/01/2015 – 31/01/2025
   • Set frequency to “Daily” or “Monthly” (daily preferred for precise aggregation)
   • Download CSV with columns:
     - Date
     - Price
     - Open
     - High
     - Low
     - Vol.
     - Change %
   • Save as gold_prices.csv

   Notes:
   • Prices are in INR per 10 grams and reflect standardized MCX contracts, capturing both global gold price changes in USD and USD/INR exchange-rate movements.[web:15][web:74]


3. DOCUMENTATION & POLICY CONTEXT (NON‑NUMERICAL SOURCES)

   • RBI communication, MOSPI press releases, and policy notes used for:
     - Describing the July 2024 gold import duty cut.
     - Providing macroeconomic context (RBI gold accumulation, inflation trends).

   Examples:
   • MOSPI CPI press releases (various months, 2015–2025)[web:50][web:57]
   • RBI Handbook of Statistics on the Indian Economy – Prices & Gold tables[web:35][web:39]
   • Articles and briefs summarizing recent gold import duty changes and RBI’s gold reserve buildup.[web:5][web:10]

All numerical analysis in the project can be replicated using only:
   • cpi_data.csv (from MOSPI)
   • gold_prices.csv (from Investing.com / MCX)
